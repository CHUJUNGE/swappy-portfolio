Swappy Project

Sustainable Exchange Platform

Swappy is a web-based platform designed to promote sustainability within the University of Melbourne community by facilitating the exchange of items between students and staff.

As the Project Coordinator, I led our team in developing this platform that operates on a token-based system, where users can upload items they no longer need, receive tokens upon approval, and then use those tokens to acquire items from other users.

Design & Development Process

Problem Definition

We identified the need for a sustainable exchange platform within the university community to reduce waste and promote reuse of items.

My role: Led stakeholder interviews and facilitated brainstorming sessions to define project scope and objectives.

User Research

Conducted surveys and interviews with potential users to understand their needs, pain points, and expectations.

My role: Designed research protocols, conducted interviews, and synthesized findings into actionable insights.

Prototyping

Created wireframes and interactive prototypes to visualize the user interface and test key user flows.

Project Reflection

What Worked Well

  • User-Centered Approach: Our focus on user needs throughout the process resulted in a platform that genuinely addresses community requirements.
  • Iterative Development: Regular feedback cycles allowed us to continuously improve the platform and address issues early.
  • Team Collaboration: Clear communication channels and regular meetings ensured everyone was aligned and working toward common goals.

Areas for Improvement

  • Mobile Optimization: While the site is responsive, we could further enhance the mobile experience with dedicated mobile-first features.
  • Backend Integration: Implementing a full backend system would enable real user accounts and persistent data storage.
  • Community Features: Adding social elements like user ratings and comments would enhance community engagement.

Personal Growth

This project significantly enhanced my skills in project coordination, user research, and technical communication. I learned how to effectively bridge the gap between technical and non-technical team members, ensuring everyone's contributions were valued and integrated into the final product.

The experience of eliciting and analyzing user feedback taught me the importance of empathetic listening and translating user needs into actionable development tasks. These skills will be invaluable in my future career as I continue to work on user-centered design projects.
